About Mufdi Zakaria:



  • born: was born on 12 June 1908. He was given the nickname of Moufdi by a school friend. Of Mozabite origin,[1] he was born and attended school in the M'zab region of Algeria.

  • His Poetry:His university education was in Tunis where he met a number of poets including Kasman. His first poetry was published in a Tunisian newspaper in 1925. Moufdi Zakaria uses implied texts in his poems through which readers can get different reflections from his poems. Quran is one of the implied texts in his works and it is strongly present in his works.[5] His poetry was believed to be largely influenced by the work of Egyptian poet Ahmed Shawqi.[6]


  • Legacy:Zakaria left his poetry and the words to Algeria's national anthem. He has postage stamps issued in his honour and Noumérat – Moufdi Zakaria Airport in Ghardaïa, Algeria is named in his honour.[7] His name was also given to cultural centre built in 1984.

  • Died:17 August 1977 (aged 69) Tunis, Tunisia

  • political and cultural activities:While he was in Tunisia and mixed with student circles there, his relationship with Abu Al-Yaqzan and the poet Ramadan Hammoud developed, and after his return to Algeria he became an active member of the North African Muslim Students Association against the policy of integration, in addition to his inclinations to the reform movement represented by the Association of Scholars, Mofdi Zakaria became involved in the North African Star Party. Then the Algerian People's Party and wrote the official party anthem "Algeria Redemption".
